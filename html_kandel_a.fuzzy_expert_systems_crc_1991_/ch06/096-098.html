The present data, although supported by a reduced number of experiments, confirm the theoretical point of view that expertise reduces the amount of information to be processed, as well as the time required to reach the desired goal. If this is proved to be true for a great number of other experiments with other types of experts, working in different physical and cultural environments, then it will be possible to assume that expert systems could also be a job for a microcomputer.

It seems possible to affirm that it is worthwhile any effort devoted to expand the present data, because of their theoretic importance and because of the new frontiers opened by the present approach.
